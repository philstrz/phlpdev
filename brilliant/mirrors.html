In the first interactive, we have four visible elements: an object in red (filled) and its image (hollow), an observer in black, and a blue line representing a mirror.

By clicking and dragging, try moving the object (red) or observer (black). Click on the image (red, hollow) to draw a line-of-sight to the image and to the original object.

In the second interactive, we have two mirrors, perpendicular to each other.

Once again, try moving the object and observer and clicking on any of the images.

In the third interactive, we again have two mirrors, but this time in parallel.

Again, try moving the object and observer and clicking on any of the images.

What if we combine the two?

Finally, what if the mirrors are neither parallel nor perpendicular?

This time, in addition to moving the object and observer and drawing lines of sight, try dragging the ends of the mirrors around.
